{% translate "Statement" %}

{% blocktranslate %} My work stems from a deep fascination with the potter's wheel and the forms that emerge from circular movement. {%endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} I am interested in rotation: that primary, repetitive, and constant gesture that allows us to think about time, form, and memory. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} In my practice, the circle and concentric forms are inevitable presences. I am drawn to their ability to reveal movement, their enveloping quality, and their insistence. They define not only the aesthetics of my pieces but embody a vision of time as something that repeats, accumulates, and intertwines. {%endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} I work with the circle as a structure open to constant transformation, that contains and, at the same time, is infinite. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} In this process, color is a tool that intervenes directly in the construction of formal language. It allows me to highlight planes, outline volumes, and mark rhythms, tracing visual accents that guide the gaze and reveal internal tensions. Color does not decorate: it activates the surface, exposes its layers, and amplifies the form. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} My work is an exploration of time as form, color as accent, and the circle as language. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% translate "BIO" %}

{% blocktranslate %} Trained as an Industrial Designer, she finds in ceramics the manifestation of her passion for manual work and crafts, and it is in the practice of the potter's wheel where she develops most of her pieces. Attention to detail, exploration of form, and the possibilities that arise from circular movement, as well as her bold use of colors, define her style. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} Her pieces are a fusion of the utilitarian, symbolic, and decorative, allowing the viewer to freely interpret their use and purpose. {% endblocktranslate %}

{% blocktranslate %} In recent years, her selection for the Mexican Utilitarian Ceramics Biennial of IVEC and her participation in the collective exhibition "1000 Vases" during Design Week in Milan stand out.
